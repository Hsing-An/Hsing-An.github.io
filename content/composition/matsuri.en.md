**A contemporary recorder work blending jazz, electronic music, and Japanese folk-inspired elements.** This is one of my most representative works.  
### This work received **Second Prize and the Best Composition Award** at Open Recorder Days Amsterdam 2023.
 <!--more-->

---
In this piece, I used the DAW [Reason](https://www.reasonstudios.com/?srsltid=AfmBOopOlDXSckGj6--NEWvOwjHG2pdTewFIFo40HpLlqW3qNLOWqujq) as my creative tool. Inspired by **odd-meter music**, I experimented with an **uneven seven-beat rhythmic structure**.  
In live performance, the piece combines **live looping** with a pre-programmed track. The recorder sound is also routed into the computer, where effects open up a wider range of timbral possibilities.

>Polygatari - 祭り(Matsuri)
>Monogatari (物語) means "story" in Japanese. Just as monophony evolves into polyphony, I wanted my monogatari to evolve as well.  
>Matsuri (祭り) means festival.  

>As the third piece in the Polygatari series, this is probably a festival taking place under a full moon, somewhere below the castle. The people here move with a staggering yet light step; he wears a white amoeba-patterned shirt, he wears a haori, he is restrained, yet free.  
>And he is very handsome.
