**Agnes Dorwarth's *Articulator I* is a contemporary work for recorder**, focusing on changes in **articulation** and **tonguing**.  
The recorder becomes the primary sound material, with the **contrast between one sound and the next** placed at the center of the listening experience.

In performance, the differences between articulations need to be handled clearly, allowing the **rhythmic drive** and **layers of timbre** to remain stable while leaving room for one's own interpretation of how the piece speaks.
